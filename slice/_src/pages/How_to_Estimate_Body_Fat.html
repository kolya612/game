How to Estimate Body Fat

Photo Weight Body Fat

Simply weighing yourself every week doesn’t provide enough information to determine your progression. For example, if you gain muscle and lose fat, your weight may stay the same; but, your body composition would be improved. It is very important to measure and log your body fat at least once per month and your weight weekly. Below, you’ll find different ways to calculate body fat. Some methods are more accurate than others. Whichever method you use, be sure you use the same method each time for accurate benchmarking.

Estimate Your Body Fat Using the Navy Seal Formula

This is not as accurate as the methods below but can provide a convenient guess-timate. For this method, you’ll need a soft tape measure and a scale. Click Here to estimate your body fat using the Navy Seal Formula.

Dual-Energy X-Ray Scan

A DEXA Scan will provide the most accurate and precise breakdown of your distribution of body fat and muscle tissue. You find out overall body fat, lean body mass, bone weight and percentages of each in each region. It also reveals potentially dangerous fat surrounding vital organs and allows you to track changes over time. It’s by far the best mechanism to objectively measure your progress. You can Google locations of DEXA Scans near you that are very common and usually reasonably priced.

Hydrostatic Method

This method calculates body fat percentage based off of body density. Imagine a water dunk tank but in a medical setting. After being weighed outside of the water, you sit in a chair that is lowered into the water. Once submerged, you exhale as much air from your lungs as possible. Your weight is then calculated. Although it’s considered a highly accurate method to measure total body fat, it will not provide distribution of fat by body part or region like DEXA. And, it’s not as convenient as some of the other methods.

Skinfold Caliper Method

Use this plier-shaped instrument to measure thickness of skin. You can order a skinfold caliper for a few dollars from Amazon. There are different formulas for this method, but we highly recommend using the 7-Site Skinfold Method for the most accurate reading. You will measure the thickness of your skin in 7 specific locations on your body and enter those measurements into the formula below to determine your total body fat percentage. This is a very accurate way to measure total body fat. It’s also more convenient and affordable than most alternatives. Click here to watch this tutorial video about how to perform a 7-point skinfold accurately. Then, click here to enter your results in this formula that determines your total body-fat percentage.

BIA (Bio Impedance Analysis)

One of the most popular types of BIA is the InBody brand. It is essentially a scale that also measures body fat. Although bio impedance is very convenient, as convenient as weighing yourself, it is definitely the least accurate method because the algorithm used to determine your body fat doesn’t actually measure your body fat but provides an estimate based upon many different variables. The problem: those variables also vary, depending on things that have nothing to do with actual body fat. However, if you use the same device each month at the same time of day, with the same level of food and water in your system, BIA can be used as a fair benchmarking tool. To determine if you’re trending in the right direction, we recommend using the same device at least once a month. Do this first thing in the morning on an empty stomach to get the most accurate reads. Bio impedance devices like InBody are very common and can be found through Google search.
